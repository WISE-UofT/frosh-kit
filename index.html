About Us

The Women in Science and Engineering â€“ University of Toronto (WISE U of T) Chapter was established in 1999 to support and empower women in STEM fields and help them achieve their full potential as future engineers, entrepreneurs, scientists, and leaders. The organization has since developed into one of the largest and highly regarded campus organizations with over 1500 members.

  • Q6. How do you allocate your time, especially for remote learning?

    Remote learning can be difficult because of the flexibility that comes with online lectures. If your professor posts lecture recordings, it can be easy to tune out or skip class. The best advice for time management in this situation is to create a schedule for yourself. Plan out regular times to watch lectures and donâ€™t forget to take breaks too to minimize screen fatigue! Once you develop a routine, it will help you stay on track and not fall behind.

  • Q7. When will we be able to join clubs around the school? Are design teams recruiting in the Fall or have they recruitment ended? Are there opportunities for us to join executive roles then?

    Yes! Check out https://www.ulife.utoronto.ca to view all official U of T student groups and recruitment opportunities. Most clubs that can function online will be running in the fall. There will also be a virtual clubs fair so stay tuned for that (see Q3)! For design teams, you can reach out to the executives listed on their website or social media. Some clubs have already finished their recruitment for the fall, however, it is still possible to join as a general member in September. This will depend on the club and it is best that you check with them (go to clubs fair)!

  • Q8. Are there any semi-formals or galas in engineering?

    In engineering, there are dinner dances for each discipline (ex. 8-Ball for track ones, ECE dinner dance) hosted by discipline clubs. However, you don't need to be part of a discpline to attend that dinner dance. Engineering also has a annual semi-formal called called Cannonball, hosted by the Engineering Society and usually held during at the end of Godiva Week.

    If you are part of an ArtSci college, colleges might also have their own galas as well. Many clubs, such as WISE, also have the International Women's Day Gala in March!

    Note: Due to COVID-19, it is uncertain that these events will occur. However, there will be many more opportunities in future years to attend!

  • Q9. The hard hat is given to all incoming engineering students, what other occasion do we get to wear it at other than F!rosh?

    There are many opportunities to wear hardhats throughout the school year, such as Hard Hat decorating or SUDS.

    If you have labs in WB25/125 (usually if you're in MIE, Civil, Mineral, Chem, or Materials Engineering) where hardhats and other protected equipment is enforced, you can wear the F!rosh hardhat (make sure to check with your professors!).

    For more information about the Skule hard hats, visit https://skulepedia.ca/wiki/Hardhats.

  • Q10. How do you find digital note-taking useful compared to manual?

    Some people find manual note-taking more useful because it helps them remember concepts better and be more present during lecture.

    Other people find digital note taking better since there are less things to carry around, easier to revise notes, and share notes with friends.

    That being said, neither form of note taking is inherently better than the other nor will they necessarily improve your productivity - it all comes down to your studying habits!

  • Q13. For commuter students, are there any beneficial meal plans available?

    Commuter meal plans are great for people who want more flexibility and freedom in their dining options. U of T has a program called Tbucks that enables you to add money to your T card and pay for food and services around campus. These dollars do not expire and can be added to your card at any time. Most commuters will be on campus during the day and will use their Tbucks for lunch, then eat dinner at home. Check out https://ueat.utoronto.ca/2018-2019-tcard/ for more information.

  • Q14. Will athletic centres be open strictly for training athletes in the Fall? Or will it be for the general student body?

    Campus athletic centers will be open for the general student body in the fall. However, there will be restrictions and modifications in place to follow health guidelines. You can check https://kpe.utoronto.ca/facilities-memberships for more details.

  • Q15. What is the Hart House usually used for?

    Hart House is a multi-purpose student center used for athletic, artistic, and wellness pursuits. Many U of T special events, debate tournaments, and career fairs are hosted here. They also have a great gym and track. Check out their website to learn more about the services they offer: https://harthouse.ca.
